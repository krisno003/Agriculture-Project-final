Step-by-Step Instructions & Best Practices

Detailed guides with practical tips for successful farming

1

Soil Testing

What: Analyze soil properties before planting.

  • 💡 Tip: Test soil pH, NPK content, and organic matter
  • 💡 Best Practice: Test annually before planting season
  • 💡 Tool: Use soil test kits or laboratory services
2

Organic Matter Addition

What: Improve soil fertility with organic materials.

  • 💡 Tip: Add 5-10 tons of compost per hectare
  • 💡 Best Practice: Incorporate 2-3 months before planting
  • 💡 Material: Use farm manure, compost, or green manure
3

pH Adjustment

What: Adjust soil pH to optimal levels for crops.

  • 💡 Tip: Most crops prefer pH 6.0-7.5
  • 💡 Best Practice: Use lime to raise pH, sulfur to lower
  • 💡 Dosage: Follow soil test recommendations
4

Field Preparation

What: Prepare land for planting with proper tilling.

  • 💡 Tip: Plough 2-3 times for fine tilth
  • 💡 Best Practice: Level field and remove weeds completely
  • 💡 Timing: Complete 2 weeks before planting
5

Mulching Application

What: Cover soil to retain moisture and suppress weeds.

  • 💡 Tip: Use 5-10 cm layer of organic mulch
  • 💡 Best Practice: Keep 15 cm away from plant stems
  • 💡 Benefits: Reduces water loss, improves soil
1

Assess Water Requirements

What: Determine crop and soil water needs.

  • 💡 Tip: Consider rainfall, evapotranspiration, and soil type
  • 💡 Best Practice: Plan irrigation schedule seasonally
  • 💡 Tool: Use weather data and soil moisture sensors
2

Choose Irrigation System

What: Select appropriate water delivery method.

  • 💡 Options: Drip, sprinkler, flood, or furrow irrigation
  • 💡 Best Practice: Drip for vegetables, sprinkler for fields
  • 💡 Efficiency: Drip saves 30-50% water vs. flood
3

Monitor Soil Moisture

What: Check soil water content regularly.

  • 💡 Tip: Squeeze test: soil holds shape but crumbles easily
  • 💡 Best Practice: Check moisture at 15cm depth
  • 💡 Frequency: Monitor every 3-5 days during growing season
4

Schedule Irrigation

What: Plan irrigation timing and frequency.

  • 💡 Tip: Irrigate during early morning or evening
  • 💡 Best Practice: Avoid irrigation during rain or extreme heat
  • 💡 Frequency: Every 5-10 days depending on crop type
5

Harvest Rainwater

What: Collect and store rainfall for dry periods.

  • 💡 Tip: Build ponds or tanks during monsoon
  • 💡 Best Practice: 1 hectare can collect 600,000 liters
  • 💡 Use: Supplement irrigation needs year-round
1

Analyze Soil Nutrients

What: Test nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium levels.

  • 💡 Tip: Get soil tested at agricultural extension office
  • 💡 Best Practice: Test every 2-3 years
  • 💡 Cost: Small fee for detailed nutrient analysis
2

Calculate Fertilizer Needs

What: Determine exact fertilizer quantities.

  • 💡 Formula: (Desired level - Current level) × Area
  • 💡 Best Practice: Follow soil test recommendations
  • 💡 Tip: Never exceed recommended doses
3

Choose Fertilizer Type

What: Select organic or inorganic fertilizers.

  • 💡 Organic: Compost, manure, bone meal (slow release)
  • 💡 Inorganic: NPK packages (quick release)
  • 💡 Best Practice: Mix both for balanced nutrition
4

Apply at Right Time

What: Time fertilizer application to crop needs.

  • 💡 Timing: Base at planting, top-dress during growth
  • 💡 Best Practice: Apply before irrigation for absorption
  • 💡 Frequency: 2-3 applications during season
5

Monitor Crop Response

What: Observe crop health and adjust if needed.

  • 💡 Yellowing: May indicate nitrogen deficiency
  • 💡 Purple leaves: Phosphorus deficiency
  • 💡 Action: Apply corrective fertilizer promptly
1

Identify Common Pests

What: Recognize pest signs and damage patterns.

  • 💡 Inspect: Check leaves, stems, and soil regularly
  • 💡 Best Practice: Scout fields weekly during season
  • 💡 Tools: Use magnifying glass for small insects
2

Use Prevention Methods

What: Stop pests before they become problems.

  • 💡 Method: Crop rotation, resistant varieties, sanitation
  • 💡 Best Practice: Remove infected plant debris promptly
  • 💡 Timing: Prevention is easier than cure
3

Apply Organic Control

What: Use natural pest management methods.

  • 💡 Methods: Neem spray, insecticidal soap, beneficial bugs
  • 💡 Best Practice: Spray early morning or late evening
  • 💡 Dose: Follow package instructions carefully
4

Monitor Results

What: Track effectiveness of pest control measures.

  • 💡 Tip: Observe pest population reduction after 5-7 days
  • 💡 Best Practice: Reapply if needed after 10-14 days
  • 💡 Record: Keep notes for future reference
5

Chemical Control (Last Resort)

What: Use pesticides only if organic methods fail.

  • 💡 Warning: Always read and follow label instructions
  • 💡 Safety: Wear protective gear while applying
  • 💡 Pre-harvest: Follow waiting period before harvest
