🌾 Grains & Cereals

Staple crops that form the foundation of global food security

Grain

Rice (Oryza sativa)

Varieties: Basmati, Jasmine, Brown, White, Parboiled
Soil: Clay-loam, pH 5.5-6.5, well-drained
Climate: Tropical, 20-35°C, high humidity
Harvest: 90-150 days, when grains turn golden

Nutritional Value (per 100g cooked)

  • Calories: 130
  • Carbs: 28g
  • Protein: 2.7g
  • Fiber: 0.4g

Uses & Benefits

Primary staple food, source of carbohydrates, gluten-free, supports digestion

Grain

Wheat (Triticum aestivum)

Varieties: Hard Red, Soft White, Durum, Spelt
Soil: Well-drained loam, pH 6.0-7.0
Climate: Temperate, 15-25°C, moderate rainfall
Harvest: 100-120 days, when grains are hard

Nutritional Value (per 100g)

  • Calories: 339
  • Carbs: 75g
  • Protein: 13g
  • Fiber: 12.2g

Uses & Benefits

Bread, pasta, breakfast cereals, gluten source, energy provider

Grain

Maize/Corn (Zea mays)

Varieties: Sweet, Dent, Flint, Popcorn, Flour
Soil: Fertile, well-drained, pH 5.8-7.0
Climate: Warm, 20-30°C, adequate moisture
Harvest: 60-100 days, when kernels are milky

Nutritional Value (per 100g)

  • Calories: 86
  • Carbs: 19g
  • Protein: 3.3g
  • Fiber: 2.7g

Uses & Benefits

Animal feed, corn oil, sweeteners, bioethanol, human consumption

🥬 Vegetables

Nutrient-rich crops essential for balanced diets and health

Vegetable

Potato (Solanum tuberosum)

Varieties: Russet, Yukon Gold, Red, Fingerling
Soil: Loose, well-drained, pH 5.0-6.5
Climate: Cool, 15-20°C, avoid frost
Harvest: 70-120 days, when vines die back

Nutritional Value (per 100g)

  • Calories: 77
  • Carbs: 17g
  • Protein: 2g
  • Vitamin C: 19.7mg

Uses & Benefits

Versatile cooking, chips, starch, animal feed, industrial uses

Vegetable

Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum)

Varieties: Beefsteak, Cherry, Roma, Heirloom
Soil: Well-drained, pH 6.0-6.8, rich organic matter
Climate: Warm, 20-25°C, full sun
Harvest: 60-80 days, when fully colored

Nutritional Value (per 100g)

  • Calories: 18
  • Carbs: 3.9g
  • Protein: 0.9g
  • Vitamin C: 14mg

Uses & Benefits

Fresh consumption, sauces, ketchup, lycopene source, antioxidant

Vegetable

Onion (Allium cepa)

Varieties: Red, White, Yellow, Sweet, Shallots
Soil: Fertile, well-drained, pH 6.0-7.0
Climate: Cool to warm, 13-24°C
Harvest: 100-120 days, when tops fall over

Nutritional Value (per 100g)

  • Calories: 40
  • Carbs: 9.3g
  • Protein: 1.1g
  • Vitamin C: 7.4mg

Uses & Benefits

Cooking flavor, medicinal properties, anti-inflammatory, heart health

Fresh fruit, juices, pickles, vitamin A source, immune booster

Energy food, potassium source, digestive health, blood pressure control

💰 Cash Crops

High-value crops grown primarily for profit and export

Cash Crop

Cotton (Gossypium)

Varieties: Upland, Pima, Egyptian, Sea Island
Soil: Well-drained, pH 5.8-8.0, fertile
Climate: Warm, 21-32°C, moderate rainfall
Harvest: 150-180 days, when bolls open

Economic Value

  • Fiber for textiles
  • Oil from seeds
  • Global trade commodity
  • High export value

Uses & Benefits

Textiles, clothing, oil production, livestock feed, industrial uses

Cash Crop

Coffee (Coffea arabica)

Varieties: Arabica, Robusta, Liberica
Soil: Well-drained, pH 6.0-6.5, volcanic
Climate: Tropical, 15-24°C, high altitude
Harvest: 3-4 years to first crop, annual thereafter

Economic Value

  • Global beverage market
  • High export earnings
  • Premium specialty coffees
  • Sustainable farming potential

Uses & Benefits

Beverage, antioxidants, employment generation, foreign exchange

Cash Crop

Tea (Camellia sinensis)

Varieties: Assam, Darjeeling, Ceylon, Green Tea
Soil: Acidic, well-drained, pH 4.5-5.5
Climate: Subtropical, 13-32°C, high rainfall
Harvest: 3-5 years to maturity, continuous plucking

Economic Value

  • Global beverage industry
  • Employment intensive
  • Export commodity
  • Health benefits market

Uses & Benefits

Beverages, antioxidants, cultural significance, rural employment
